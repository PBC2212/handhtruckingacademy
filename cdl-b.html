What You'll Learn

CDL-B Curriculum

A structured, 3-week program combining classroom instruction with extensive behind-the-wheel training.

Week 1

Foundations & Classroom

Build your knowledge base with comprehensive classroom instruction covering regulations, safety, and vehicle systems.

  • CDL regulations and DOT compliance
  • Vehicle inspection procedures (pre-trip & post-trip)
  • Air brake systems and operation
  • Traffic laws and defensive driving principles
  • Cargo securement and weight distribution
Week 2

Yard Skills & Maneuvering

Transition from classroom to hands-on practice with controlled yard exercises and basic road skills.

  • Straight-line backing and offset backing
  • Parallel parking with commercial vehicles
  • Turning techniques for intersections and tight spaces
  • Coupling and uncoupling procedures
  • Controlled speed management and braking
Week 3

Road Training & Exam Prep

Hit the open road with supervised driving in real-world traffic conditions and prepare for your CDL-B exam.

  • City driving and urban navigation
  • Highway merging and lane management
  • Hazard recognition and emergency procedures
  • Night driving and adverse weather techniques
  • CDL-B skills test and road test preparation
Ongoing

Support & Job Placement

Your training doesn't end at graduation. We provide continued support to help you launch your driving career.

  • Resume building and interview preparation
  • Employer introductions and job matching
  • CDL exam scheduling assistance
  • Post-graduation refresher sessions
  • Career counseling and advancement guidance
Vehicles You Can Drive

Class B Vehicle Types

A CDL-B license qualifies you to operate a wide range of single-unit commercial vehicles.

🚛

Straight Trucks

Box trucks and delivery vehicles used for local and regional freight transport. High demand in logistics and e-commerce.

🚍

City & Transit Buses

Public transit buses, shuttle buses, and charter vehicles. Steady employment with municipal and private operators.

🏗

Dump Trucks

Construction and landscaping dump trucks. Essential for the booming construction industry across Texas.
